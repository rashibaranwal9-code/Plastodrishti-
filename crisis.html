The Invisible Threat in Our Waters

Mismanaged plastic waste breaks down over centuries into microscopic toxic particles, completely choking global aquatic ecosystems, rivers, and freshwater lakes.

Choked River Networks

Plastic debris accumulating directly in freshwater river paths, physically degrading water quality and fragmenting into microplastics.

Suffocated Lakes

Floating urban garbage and heavy algal blooms trapping industrial particles, making local water dangerous for human or ecosystem use.
